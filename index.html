About Me

Welcome to my portfolio- I'm Andrew Frost and this website is a showcase of projects that I've worked on. Be sure to check the Projects page to see an overview, browse the source code, and play around with anything that catches your eye!

I've always had an interest in video games, but didn't decide to pursue it as a career until a little later in life. I first started programming in late 2012 by following some online JavaScript tutorials, and I liked it enough to pursue a degree in programming. I began my studies at Texas A&M University - Corpus Christi in Fall of 2013 where I was first introduced to C++ and got to delve deeper into programming. I gained a lot of experience at college and would recommend it for most people interested in programming, but it's far from the only way to learn. I graduated Cum Laude and received a Bachelor of Science Degree in Game Programming in May of 2016.

After graduating, I've been busy pursuing a few other opportunities, but in my free time I've been working on a game engine along with implementing a game in it, as well as a variety of small programming projects.

I love making programs that surprise people at how fast they work- there's something very satisfying about a tool that does its job spectacularly with no useless bloat.

If you're a recruiter in the Austin area looking for a strong C++ programmer, you can contact me by email at andrewdylanfrost@gmail.com.

Other

Weather On Wheels

A project I contributed to at the Conrad Blucher Institute (CBI) that would give forecasted weather and severe weather events along a travel route. I worked with an existing codebase to improve speed, reliability, and implement additional features such as optional overlays for temperatures, precipitation levels, and windspeeds along a route. Weather On Wheels was available on a website and in the Android and Apple stores, but unfortunately, CBI no longer maintains the project and handed it off to another company that has left it nonfunctioning for a long time.

A poster I used for a presentation on Weather On Wheels that contains some additional information and screenshots of it in a late stage of development can be found here (7.8 MB).
